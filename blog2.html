The Art of Negotiation: Recovering Public Assets for the Common Good


In today's rapidly evolving political and economic landscape, the recovery of public assets that have been lost, misappropriated, or poorly managed is one of the most pressing issues for governments around the world. At the heart of this challenge lies the art of negotiation. Barr. Gilbert Agbor, a seasoned legal expert and public advocate, has dedicated much of his career to the strategic recovery of public assets through careful and tactical negotiation, aiming to return these resources to the people they rightfully belong to. Public assets are often the lifeblood of a community, supporting critical infrastructure such as healthcare, education, transportation, and utilities. When these assets are lost or misused—whether through corruption, financial mismanagement, or neglect—it is the citizens who suffer the most.
Barr. Agbor has consistently emphasized the need for strong negotiation strategies in the pursuit of asset recovery. "Recovering public assets is not just about reclaiming money or property—it’s about restoring the public’s faith in governance," he explains.
Negotiating for the recovery of public assets requires a unique blend of diplomacy, legal acumen, and strategic foresight. Barr. Agbor's approach to asset recovery hinges on three key principles: transparency, collaboration, and persistence.

Transparency: Building Trust through Open Negotiations

Transparency is fundamental to Barr. Agbor’s negotiation style. Whether dealing with local actors or international stakeholders, he ensures that the process is clear and accountable at every stage. "People need to know that we are negotiating in their best interests," he asserts. "Openness in the negotiation process builds trust not only with the public but also with those on the other side of the table."

Negotiations often occur behind closed doors, but Barr. Agbor has always advocated for public accountability, especially when it involves assets that affect the livelihoods of citizens. He ensures that his negotiation strategies are aligned with legal standards and that the public is kept informed about progress and outcomes, especially when it involves major public assets like land, funds, or government contracts.

Collaboration: Engaging Stakeholders in the Recovery Process

No asset recovery effort can succeed in isolation. Barr. Agbor stresses the importance of collaboration in the negotiation process, working closely with government bodies, private organizations, international partners, and local communities. "Negotiation is not just about legal expertise; it’s about understanding the needs of all stakeholders and finding common ground," he says.
Recovering public assets often involves engaging with multiple parties, including foreign governments, international financial institutions, and private entities that may have taken control of public resources. Barr. Agbor’s ability to bring different players to the table—sometimes with conflicting interests—is one of his greatest strengths. He is known for fostering dialogue, where solutions are developed collectively and beneficially for all involved.
In one notable case, Barr. Agbor led negotiations for the recovery of land that had been unlawfully seized by private interests. By working closely with local government officials, community leaders, and the private sector, he was able to reach an agreement that returned the land to public use while ensuring compensation for all affected parties. "It’s about creating win-win situations where the public good is prioritized without compromising fairness," he emphasizes.

It often involves facing opposition from powerful interests that stand to lose significant financial or political leverage. However, Barr. Agbor’s persistence in the face of these challenges has made him a formidable negotiator.
